During my internship at the Unit Hal Ehwal Islam (UHEI) at Universiti Teknologi MARA (UiTM), I gained valuable experience in various office tasks and learned a lot about administrative work. My main duties included creating and editing posters, managing official letters, and shredding documents. I also helped prepare reports for programs and drafted meeting minutes, which helped me improve my writing and organizational skills. Working at UHEI taught me how to be more responsible and detail-oriented in handling tasks. I was also able to develop my communication skills as I worked closely with my colleagues and supervisors. This internship was a great opportunity for me to understand the importance of office work and how it supports the smooth running of an organization. Overall, my time at UHEI was a positive learning experience that allowed me to grow both personally and professionally.
However, if I were to recommend this place for students at UiTM to intern, I would say it might not be the best choice. This is because the internship took place during the semester break, and as a result, there were fewer students around, and fewer programs took place. This meant there were fewer opportunities for hands-on learning and interaction with students. However, during regular times when students return from semester breaks, the situation changes. UHEI is involved in a lot of activities, and interns have the chance to participate in various programs, events, and projects. The office is busier, and interns would have more opportunities to engage in meaningful tasks. Therefore, if a student from outside UiTM were to intern here, I would definitely recommend it, as they would have a fuller experience during the active periods.
